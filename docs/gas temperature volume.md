**# Gas temperature & volume (Charles' law)

In an ideal gas:

> The [[volume]] of a [[gas]] is [[direct proportion|directly proportional]] to its [[absolute temperature]]

## Formulae

We can write Charles' law in two ways:

> $$
> \frac VT=\text{constant}
> $$

- $V$ is the volume of the gas
- $T$ is the temperature of the gas **in [[kelvin]]** ([[absolute temperature]])

> $$
> \frac{V_1}{T_1}=\frac{V_2}{T_2}
> $$

## [[flashcards]]

| Question | Answer |
| --- | --- |
| What is Charles' law for an ideal gas? | The volume of a gas is directly proportional to its absolute temperature. |
| How is $\frac{V}{T} = \text{constant}$ written? | $V$ is volume, $T$ is temperature in kelvin (absolute temperature). |
| What is the formula $\frac{V_1}{T_1} = \frac{V_2}{T_2}$ used for? | Relating initial and final volume and temperature for a gas. |
| What temperature scale must be used in Charles' law? | Kelvin (absolute temperature). |
