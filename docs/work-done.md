# Work done

*Work* is *done* when a force is applied to an object to make it move.

Work done is the same as the *energy transferred* to the object.

> Anywhere where we have a change of energy (e.g. in all the kinetic, gravitational potential, etc. energy formulas) we can replace the energy with work done!

## Units

Because work done is just another way of saying energy transferred, it has the same units: joules (J).

## $W=Fs$

- $W$ is the work done, in [[joule]]s ($J$)
- $F$ is the [[force]] applied, in [[newton]]s ($N$)
- $s$ is the [[displacement]], in [[metre]]s ($m$)

## $W=Fs\cos\theta$

- $W$ is the work done, in [[joule]]s ($J$)
- $F$ is the [[force]] applied, in [[newton]]s ($N$)
- $s$ is the [[displacement]], in [[metre]]s ($m$)
- $\theta$ is the **direction** of the [[displacement]]

## Energy transfer

Importantly, the *type* of energy often changes. For example, the energy may be transferred from chemical energy in fuel to kinetic energy of a moving car.
