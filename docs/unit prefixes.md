# Unit prefixes

By adding a decimal unit prefix after a unit, we change the *magnitude*
([[power]] of 10) of the unit/amount.

For example, 1 **kilo**metre (km) is $1000$ metres (m), or $10^3$ metres.

| Prefix | Symbol | Factor        |
|--------|--------|---------------|
| femto  | f      | $10^{-15}$    |
| pico   | p      | $10^{-12}$    |
| nano   | n      | $10^{-9}$     |
| micro  | µ      | $10^{-6}$     |
| milli  | m      | $10^{-3}$     |
| centi  | c      | $10^{-2}$     |
| deci   | d      | $10^{-1}$     |
| kilo   | k      | $10^{3}$      |
| mega   | M      | $10^{6}$      |
| giga   | G      | $10^{9}$      |
| tera   | T      | $10^{12}$     |
| peta   | P      | $10^{15}$     |

## Example conversions

### Example: Convert 5 kilometres to metres.

- 1 kilometre (km) = $10^3$ metres (m)
- Therefore, 5 km = $5 \times 10^3$ m = 5000 m

### Example: Convert 2500 milligrams to grams.

- 1 milligram (mg) = $10^{-3}$ grams (g)
- Therefore, 2500 mg = $2500 \times 10^{-3}$ g = $2.5 g$

### Example: Convert 3 megabytes to bytes.

- 1 megabyte (MB) = $10^{6}$ bytes (B)
- Therefore, 3 MB = $3 \times 10^{6}$ B = $3,000,000$ B

### Example: Convert 0.75 gigawatts to watts.

- 1 gigawatt (GW) = $10^{9}$ watts (W)
- Therefore, 0.75 GW = $0.75 \times 10^{9}$ W = $750,000,000$ W

### Example: Convert 120 centimeters to meters.
- 1 centimeter (cm) = $10^{-2}$ meters (m)
- Therefore, 120 cm = $120 \times 10^{-2}$ m = $1.2$ m

### Example: Convert 0.005 terabytes to megabytes.
- 1 terabyte (TB) = $10^{12}$ bytes (B)
- 1 megabyte (MB) = $10^{6}$ bytes (B)
- Therefore, 0.005 TB = $0.005 \times 10^{12}$ B = $5 \times 10^{9}$ B = $5000$ MB

### Example: Convert 732 femtometers to meters

- 1 femtometer (fm) = $10^{-15}$ meters (m)
- Therefore, 732 fm = $732 \times 10^{-15}$ m = $7.32 \times 10^{-13}$ m
