# Electron repulsion

It's possible for two [[electron]]s to both [[repulsion|repel]] each other, if they are moving towards each other and they get close enough.

## Photon emission

In this case, both of the electrons emit a [[photon]] each, which is the [[exchange particle]] which has the force to cause the two electrons to repel.

## [[flashcards]]

| Question | Answer |
| --- | --- |
| What happens when two electrons moving towards each other get close enough? | They both emit a photon each, which is the exchange particle causing repulsion. |
| What is the exchange particle for electron-electron repulsion? | A photon. |
