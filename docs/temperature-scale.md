# Temperature scale

There are two main [[temperature]] scales (unless you're american):
- [[degree-celsius]], $^\circ C$
- [[kelvin]], $K$

## Conversions

The scale of both are the same, but they 'start' from different temperatures.

### Converting celsius to kelvin

For a [[temperature]] in [[degree-celsius]], you **add** $273(.15)$ to the celsius value to get the [[kelvin]] value.

### Converting kelvin to celsius 

For a [[temperature]] in [[kelvin]], you **subtract** $273(.15)$ to the [[kelvin]] value. to get the [[degree-celsius]] value.

## Usage in formulae

- A $\theta$ in a formula represents a [[temperature]] in [[degree-celsius]].
- A $T$ in a formula represents a [[temperature]] in [[kelvin]].
