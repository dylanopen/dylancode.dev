# Hooke's law

> The [[extension]] of a [[spring]] is [[direct proportion|directly proportional]] to the [[force]] applied, until the [[limit of proportionality]] is reached.

# Equation

We can write Hooke's law, up until the [[limit of proportionality]], as:

> $F=k\Delta L$

- $F$ is the [[force]] applied, in [[newton]]s ($N$)
- $k$ is the [[spring constant]] (or stiffness constant) in [[newton]]s per [[metre]] ($Nm^{-1}$)
- $\Delta L$ is the [[extension]] of the spring, in [[metre]]s.

$\text{force}=\text{spring constant}\times\text{extension}$
