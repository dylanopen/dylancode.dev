# Units of energy

There are two main units of energy:
- Joules - what you're used to seeing.
- Kilowatt-hours (kWh) - often shown in things like electricity bills.

## Joules

One joule is one watt-second, which means it's the energy transferred when one
watt of [[power]] is applied for one second.

## Kilowatt-hours

One $kWh$ is the energy transferred when one kilowatt (1000 watts) of [[power]] is applied for one hour (3600 seconds).

$$
1 \text{ kWh} = 1000 \text{ W} \times 3600 \text{ s} = 3,600,000 \text{ J}
$$

## Converting between joules and kilowatt-hours

1 kilowatt-hour (kWh) is equal to 3,600,000 joules (J).

$$
1 \text{ kWh} = 3,600,000 \text{ J}
$$
