## [[flashcards]]

| Question | Answer |
| --- | --- |
| Maths - Disguised Logarithm Equations | How do you solve $\log_5{125}$?	$\log_5{125} = 3$ because $5^3 = 125$<br/>This is a disguised logarithm equation where the argument is a power of the base. |
| Maths - Exponential Graphs | What shape does an exponential graph $y = a^x$ have?	An exponential graph increases rapidly for $a>1$; it passes through $(0,1)$ and approaches the x-axis as $x$ tends to negative infinity. |
| Maths - Exponential Graph Gradient | How do you find the gradient of an exponential graph at a point?	The gradient is given by the derivative $dy/dx = a^x \ln(a)$ for $y=a^x$. |
| Maths - Base e Exponentials | What is the function $y = e^x$?	$y = e^x$ is an exponential function with base $e$, where $e \approx 2.71828$. Its derivative is itself ($dy/dx = e^x$). |
| Maths - Converting Exponentials to Base e | How do you convert $a^x$ to base $e$?	$a^x = e^{x \ln(a)}$. |
| Maths - Natural Logarithm Graphs | What is the shape of $y = \ln(x)$?	$y = \ln(x)$ is the inverse of $y = e^x$; it passes through $(1,0)$, increases slowly, and has a vertical asymptote at $x=0$. |
| Maths - Exponential Growth | What defines exponential growth?	Exponential growth occurs when a quantity increases at a rate proportional to its current value, modeled by $y = A e^{kt}$ with $k>0$. |
| Maths - Exponential Decay | What defines exponential decay?	Exponential decay occurs when a quantity decreases at a rate proportional to its current value, modeled by $y = A e^{-kt}$ with $k>0$. |
| Maths - Compound Interest | How is compound interest calculated?	$A = P(1 + r/n)^{nt}$, where $P$ is principal, $r$ is annual rate, $n$ is number of compounding periods per year, $t$ is time in years. |
| Maths - Logarithmic Scale Graphs | What is a logarithmic scale graph?	A graph where one or both axes use a logarithmic scale, so equal distances represent multiplicative changes (e.g., orders of magnitude). |
| Maths - Binomial Expansion Definition | What is a binomial expansion?	The expansion of $(a+b)^n$ into a sum of terms involving powers of $a$ and $b$ with binomial coefficients. |
| Maths - Binomial Combination | What is $\binom{n}{r}$?	$\binom{n}{r}$ is the binomial coefficient, equal to $\frac{n!}{r!(n-r)!}$, representing the number of combinations of $n$ items taken $r$ at a time. |
| Maths - Common Binomial Values | What are common binomial values?	$\binom{n}{0} = 1$, $\binom{n}{1} = n$, $\binom{n}{n-1} = n$, $\binom{n}{n} = 1$. |
| Maths - Binomial Expansion of a Single Term | How is $(1+x)^n$ expanded for small $x$?	$(1+x)^n = 1 + nx + \frac{n(n-1)}{2!}x^2 + \dots$ for $|x|<1$. |
| Maths - Binomial Theorem | State the Binomial Theorem.	$(a+b)^n = \sum_{r=0}^n \binom{n}{r} a^{n-r} b^r$. |
| Maths - Binomial Approximation | When is binomial approximation used?	For $(1+x)^n$ when $|x|$ is small; using first few terms provides an approximation. |
| Maths - Binomial Expansion Using Factorials | How is $\binom{n}{r}$ expressed using factorials?	$\binom{n}{r} = \frac{n!}{r!(n-r)!}$. |
| Physics - Gravitational Acceleration | What is gravitational acceleration?	The acceleration of an object due to gravity near Earth's surface, approximately $g = 9.81 \, \text{m/s}^2$ downward. |
| Physics - Free Fall | What is free fall?	Motion under the influence of gravity only, with no other forces like air resistance. |
| Physics - Experiment to Calculate g | How is $g$ measured experimentally?	Using methods like timing a falling object or using a pendulum, calculating $g$ from $T = 2\pi\sqrt{L/g}$. |
| Physics - Projectile Motion | What is projectile motion?	Motion of an object under gravity with an initial velocity; it follows a parabolic path in two dimensions (horizontal and vertical). |
| Physics - Vertical Projectile Motion | What characterizes vertical projectile motion?	Only vertical motion under gravity; vertical acceleration $= -g$, vertical velocity changes linearly. |
| Physics - Horizontal Projectile Motion | What is horizontal projectile motion?	Initial velocity is horizontal; horizontal velocity is constant, vertical motion is accelerated downward by $g$. |
| Physics - Angled Projectile Motion | What is angled projectile motion?	Initial velocity at an angle; horizontal component constant, vertical component has acceleration $g$. |
| Physics - Friction | What is friction?	A force opposing relative motion between surfaces, proportional to normal force; $F_f \le \mu N$. |
| Physics - Drag | What is drag?	A force resisting motion through a fluid (air or water), depending on speed, area, and fluid density. |
| Physics - Lift | What is lift?	A force perpendicular to the direction of motion in a fluid, generated by pressure differences (e.g., on wings). |
| Physics - Terminal Velocity | What is terminal velocity?	The constant speed reached when drag equals gravitational force, resulting in zero net acceleration. |
| Physics - First Law of Motion | State Newton's First Law of Motion.	An object remains at rest or in uniform motion unless acted upon by a net external force. |
| Physics - Second Law of Motion | State Newton's Second Law of Motion.	$F = ma$; net force equals mass times acceleration. |
| Physics - Third Law of Motion | State Newton's Third Law of Motion.	For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction; forces occur in pairs. |
| Physics - Momentum | What is momentum?	$p = mv$, the product of mass and velocity; a vector quantity. |
| Physics - Momentum and Force | How are momentum and force related?	$F = \Delta p / \Delta t$, net force equals rate of change of momentum. |
| Physics - Impulse | What is impulse?	$J = F \Delta t = \Delta p$, change in momentum due to a force over time. |
| Physics - Conservation of Momentum | State conservation of momentum.	In a closed system, total momentum before an interaction equals total momentum after. |
| Physics - Elastic Collision | What is an elastic collision?	A collision where both momentum and kinetic energy are conserved. |
| Physics - Inelastic Collision | What is an inelastic collision?	A collision where momentum is conserved but kinetic energy is not; objects may stick together. |
| Physics - Explosion Momentum | How does momentum apply in an explosion?	Total initial momentum is zero; after explosion, the total momentum of fragments sums to zero. |
| Physics - Density | What is density?	$\rho = m/V$, mass per unit volume. |
| Physics - Work Done | What is work done?	$W = F \cdot d$ for constant force in direction of displacement; energy transferred by a force. |
| Physics - Calculating Work Done | How is work done calculated?	$W = Fd \cos \theta$, where $\theta$ is the angle between force and displacement. |
| Physics - Work Done at an Angle | How do you calculate work when force is at an angle?	$W = Fd \cos \theta$, only the component of force in the direction of displacement does work. |
| Physics - Force-Displacement Graphs | What does area under a force-displacement graph represent?	The area equals the work done by the force. |
| Physics - Power | What is power?	$P = W/t = Fv$, rate of doing work or transferring energy. |
| Physics - Conservation of Energy | State conservation of energy.	Energy cannot be created or destroyed, only transformed from one form to another. |
| Physics - Efficiency | What is efficiency?	Efficiency = (useful output energy / total input energy) $\times 100\%$. |
| Physics - Energy Sources | What are examples of energy sources?	Renewable (solar, wind, hydro) and non-renewable (coal, oil, gas, nuclear). |
| Physics - Energy Forms | What are forms of energy?	Kinetic, potential (gravitational, elastic), thermal, chemical, nuclear, electrical, light, sound. |
| Physics - Kinetic Energy | What is kinetic energy?	$KE = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$, energy due to motion. |
| Physics - Gravitational Potential Energy | What is gravitational potential energy?	$GPE = mgh$, energy due to position in a gravitational field. |
| Physics - Elastic Potential Energy | What is elastic potential energy?	$EPE = \frac{1}{2}kx^2$ for a spring, energy stored due to deformation. |
