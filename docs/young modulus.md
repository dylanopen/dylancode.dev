# Young modulus

- The Young [[modulus]] determines how difficult it is to change an object's shape.
    - A small Young [[modulus]] means it is **easier** to change its shape.
    - A large Young [[modulus]] means it is **harder** to change its shape.

## Stiffness

- A **large** young [[modulus]] means it is very **hard** to stretch a material.

## Young modulus equation

> $$
> E=\frac\sigma\epsilon
> $$


- $E$ is the Young [[modulus]] of the material.
- $\sigma$ is the [[stress]].
- $\epsilon$ is the [[strain]].

We can also derive another equation, knowing the equations for [[stress]] and [[strain]].

> $$
> E=\frac{F\times L}{\Delta L\times A}
> $$
