# Binomial distribution formula

If we know $X\sim B(n,p)$:

> $$
> P(X=x)=^nC_x\space p^x (1-p)^{n-x}
> $$

- $P(X=x)$ is the probability of getting exactly $x$ successes.
- $p$ is the probability of any single trial being successful.
- $n$ is the number of trials
- $x$ is the exact number of successes we are checking the probability of.

Remember that $^nC_x$ is a [[binomial combination]].
