# Unstable nuclei

## What makes a [[nucleus]] unstable?

The [[strong-nuclear-force]] has a very small range, which, for small [[isotope]]s, is fine. But when the [[nucleus]] gets bigger, the strong nuclear force can't hold the nucleus together as well, and it becomes unstable.

It can't hold it together because of its short range - some protons are more than $3 \times 10^{-15}$ metres apart, which is its maximum range.
