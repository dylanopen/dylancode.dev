# Conic section transformations

There are a few different transformations we can apply to a [[conic section]].

## Parabola translations

Whenever a [[parabola]] is [[translation|translated]], we can just replace $x$ with $x-h$ and $y$ with $y-k$, where $(h,k)$ is the translation vector.

### Translate the parabola $y^2=4ax$ by the vector $(h, k)$ and find its new focus.

- Replace $x$ with $x-h$ and $y$ with $y-k$:
  - $(y-k)^2=4a(x-h)$
- The original focus was at $(a,0)$
- It's translated by $(h, k)$, so the new focus is at $(a+h, k)$.

### Translate the parabola $y^2=8x$ by the vector $(9, -8)$ and find its new focus

- Replace $x$ with $x-9$ and $y$ with $y+8$:
  - $(y+8)^2=4a(x-9)$
- The original vertex was at $(0,0)$
- It's translated by $(9, -8)$, so the new vertex is at $(9,-8)$.

- The original focus was at $(2,0)$
- It's translated by $(9, -8)$, so the new focus is at $(11, -8)$.

## [[flashcards]]

| Question | Answer |
| --- | --- |
| What is the general substitution for translating a parabola by vector $(h, k)$? | Replace $x$ with $x-h$ and $y$ with $y-k$. |
| How do you find the new focus of a parabola $y^2=4ax$ after translation by $(h, k)$? | Add $(h, k)$ to original focus $(a,0)$, giving $(a+h, k)$. |
| Translate $y^2=4ax$ by $(h, k)$ using substitution. | $(y-k)^2=4a(x-h)$ |
| What is the new equation when $y^2=8x$ is translated by $(9, -8)$? | $(y+8)^2=8(x-9)$ |
| What is the new vertex when $y^2=8x$ is translated by $(9, -8)$? | $(9, -8)$ (original vertex $(0,0)$ translated) |
| What is the new focus when $y^2=8x$ is translated by $(9, -8)$? | $(11, -8)$ (original focus $(2,0)$ plus $(9, -8)$) |
