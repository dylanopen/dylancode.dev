# Stationary wave

A *stationary wave* is a wave which doesn't actually transfer energy.

That's because it's made up of two waves in opposite directions! The waves have:
- the same frequency
- the same [[amplitude]]
- the same [[speed]]
- the same [[wavelength]]

They *interfere* with each other, which produces some key features, which we'll
look at in the next sections.

> Stationary waves are waves made up of two of the same waves in opposite
> directions interfering to proudce [[wave node|nodes]] and
> [[wave antinode|antinodes]]. They don't transfer energy.
