# Laws of logarithms

There are some important laws of logarithms that are useful to know.
These can be derived from the [[laws of indices]].

These laws tell us how to:
- [[adding logarithms|add logarithms]]
- [[subtracting logarithms|subtract logarithms]]
- [[multiplying logarithms by a constant|multiply logarithms by a constant]]

## [[flashcards]]

| Question | Answer |
| --- | --- |
| What is the relationship between the laws of logarithms and the laws of indices? | The laws of logarithms can be derived from the laws of indices. |
| What operation corresponds to $\log A + \log B$? | It corresponds to $\log (A \times B)$. |
| What operation corresponds to $\log A - \log B$? | It corresponds to $\log \left( \frac{A}{B} \right)$. |
| What is the effect of multiplying a logarithm by a constant $n$, i.e. $n \log A$? | It corresponds to $\log (A^n)$. |
