---
title: Self-base logarithms
---

Whenever we have a logarithm that's in the form:

$$
\log_a{a^x}
$$

...the $a$s essentially cancel out, and we just get $x$ as the answer.

$$
\log_a{a^x}=x
$$

### Evaluate $\log_5{5^3}$

- 5 to the power of what makes 5 to the power of 3?
- Well... 3.
- So our answer is $3$!

### Evaluate $\log_{10}{10^{-2}}$

- $10^x=10^{-2}$
- $x=-2$
- Answer: $-2$

### Evaluate $\log_2{2^{\frac12}}$

- $2^x=2^{\frac12}$
- $x=\frac12$
- Answer: $\frac12$

### Evaluate $\log_3{3^0}$

- $3^x=3^0$
- $x=0$
- Answer: $0$

### Evaluate $\log_7{7}$

- $7^x=7^1$
- $x=1$
- Answer: $1$

### Simplify $\log_4{4^{2x+1}}$

- $4^{y}=4^{2x+1}$
- $y=2x+1$
- Answer: $2x+1$

## Natural logarithms of $e$

$\ln x$ just means $\log_e x$, so $\ln{e^x}$ is just $\log_e{e^x}$, which is
just $x$.

$$
\ln{e^x}=x
$$

### Evaluate $\ln{e^4}$

- $=\log_e{e^4}$
- $e^x=e^4$
- $x=4$
- Answer: $4$

### Evaluate $\ln{e^{-3}}$

- $\ln{e^x}=x$
- $x=-3$
- Answer: $-3$

### Evaluate $\ln{e^{\frac{x}{2}}}$

- $\ln{e^x}=x$
- $x=\frac{x}{2}$
- Answer: $\frac x2$

### Evaluate $\ln{e^0}$

- $\ln{e^x}=x$
- $x=0$
- Answer: $0$

### Evaluate $\ln{e}$

- $\ln{e^x}=x$
- $e^x=e^1$
- $x=1$
- Answer: $1$
