---
title: Dividing indices
---

When we divide two indices **with the same base**, we can simply **subtract**
the powers. This can be written as:

$$
a^x\div a^b=a^{x-y}
$$

### Example: simplify $x^3\div x^{-2}$

- $=x^{3--2}$
- $=x^{3+2}$
- $=x^5$

### Example: evaluate $2^7\div 2^3$

- $=2^{7-3}$
- $=2^4$
- $=16$

### Example: evaluate $5^3\div 5^0$

- $=5^{3-0}$
- $=5^3$
- $=125$

### Example: simplify $y^4\div y$

- $=y^{4-1}$
- $=y^3$
