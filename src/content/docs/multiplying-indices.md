---
title: Multiplying indices
---

When we multiply two indices **with the same base**, we can simply **add** the
powers. This can be written as:

$$
a^x\times a^b=a^{x+y}
$$

### Example: simplify $x^3\times x^{-2}$

- $=x^{3+-2}$
- $=x^{3-2}$
- $=x^1$
- $=x$

### Example: evaluate $2^4\times 2^3$

- $=2^{4+3}$
- $=2^7$
- $=128$

### Example: evaluate $5^0\times 5^3$

- $=5^{0+3}$
- $=5^3$
- $=125$

### Example: evaluate $7^2\times 7^{-5}$

- $=7^{2+-5}$
- $=7^{-3}$
- $=\frac{1}{7^3}$
- $=\frac{1}{343}$

### Example: simplify $y\times y^{-4}$
- $=y^{1+-4}$
- $=y^{-3}$ or $\frac{1}{y^3}$
