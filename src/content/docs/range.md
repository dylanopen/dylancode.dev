---
title: Range
---

If we have a set of values, the **range** is the difference between the largest
and smallest values in the set.

## Calculating range

> $$
> \text{range} = \text{largest value} - \text{smallest value}
> $$

## Examples

### Find the range of $\{2, 3, 5, 7, 11\}$

- $\text{largest value} = 11$
- $\text{smallest value} = 2$
- $\text{range} = \text{largest value} - \text{smallest value}$
- $\text{range} = 11 - 2$
- **Answer**: $\text{range} = 9$

### Find the range of $\{1, 4, 9, 16, 25\}$

- $\text{largest value} = 25$
- $\text{smallest value} = 1$
- $\text{range} = \text{largest value} - \text{smallest value}$
- $\text{range} = 25 - 1$
- **Answer**: $\text{range} = 24$

### Find the range of $\{3, 1, 4, 1, 5, 9\}$

- $\text{largest value} = 9$
- $\text{smallest value} = 1$
- $\text{range} = \text{largest value} - \text{smallest value}$
- $\text{range} = 9 - 1$
- **Answer**: $\text{range} = 8$
