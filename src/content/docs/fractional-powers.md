---
title: Fractional powers
---

When we raise a number to a fraction, the **denominator** of the fraction is the
**order of the root**.

$$
a^{\frac xy}=(\sqrt[y]{a})^b
$$

### Example: evaluate $16^{\frac34}$

- $=(\sqrt[4]{16})^3$
- $=2^3$
- $=8$

### Example: evaluate $27^{\frac23}$

- $=(\sqrt[3]{27})^2$
- $=3^2$
- $=9$

### Example: write $x^{\frac13}$ in surd form

- $=\sqrt[3]{x}$

### Example: write $a^{\frac25}$ in surd form

- $=(\sqrt[5]{a})^2$

### Example: evaluate $81^{\frac12}$

- $=\sqrt{81}$
- $=9$
