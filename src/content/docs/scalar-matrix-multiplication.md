---
title: Matrix scalar multiplication
---

When multiplying a matrix by a scalar (a single number), we multiply each element
of the matrix by that scalar.

For a 2x2 matrix, this can be written as:

$$
k 
\begin{bmatrix}
a & b \\
c & d
\end{bmatrix} =
\begin{bmatrix}
k a & k b \\
k c & k d
\end{bmatrix}
$$

## Examples

### Evaluate $2 \times \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 3 \\ 5 & 7 \end{bmatrix}$

- multiply each element of the matrix by 2:
  - Top-left: $2 \times 1 = 2$
  - Top-right: $2 \times 3 = 6$
  - Bottom-left: $2 \times 5 = 10$
  - Bottom-right: $2 \times 7 = 14$
- So, $2 \times \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 3 \\ 5 & 7 \end{bmatrix} =
\begin{bmatrix} 2 & 6 \\ 10 & 14 \end{bmatrix}$
- **Answer**: $\begin{bmatrix} 2 & 6 \\ 10 & 14 \end{bmatrix}$
