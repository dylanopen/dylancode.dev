---
title: Analogue signal
---

An analogue signal is effectively a *wave*, in the form you've probably seen
before. They take on *continuous values* - that just means that an analogue
signal at a point may represent the value 0.7, 0.421, or any other value
between 0 and 1!

> Analogue signals store **continuous values**.

Computers don't work with analogue signals, so they need to be converted into a
digital signal if we want to use them in a computer.
