---
title: Bitmap resolution
---

The resolution of a bitmap image is the *width* and *height* of the image. More
Specifically, it is the *number of pixels* in the image.

For example, if an image is 300 pixels wide and 200 pixels in height, then we
might say it has a resolution of `300x200`, or possibly `60,000` pixels.

## Calculating resolution

If we know the width and the height of an image, we can calculate its resolution
using:

> $$
> \text{resolution} = \text{image width}\times\text{image height}
> $$
