Velocity

Velocity measures how much the displacement changes, every second.

It measures the rate of change of displacement in a specific direction, so it is a vector quantity.

Example

  • We walk 25km north in 5 hours, so to find our velocity:

  • displacement = 25km north

  • velocity = displacement / time

  • v=25km\div5h=5kmh^{-1}

  • Or, if we walk 400m north then 500m south in 200s, finding our velocity:

  • displacement = 400m-500m=100m south

  • v=s\div t

  • v=100m\div200s=0.5ms^{-1} south

\text{velocity}=\text{displacement}\div\text{time}

s=vt

v=\frac st

Where:

  • s is the displacement, measured in metres, m
  • v is the velocity, measured in metres per second, ms^{-1} (m/s)
  • t is the time, measured in seconds, s

A van drives 200m in 10s. Calculate the velocity.

  • v=\frac st
  • v=\frac{200}{10}=20
  • Answer: 20ms^{-1}

A car drives 75m in 5s. Calculate the velocity.

  • v=\frac st
  • v=\frac{75}{5}=15
  • Answer: 15ms^{-1}

  • displacement = 150m-50m=100m north
  • v=\frac st
  • v=\frac{100}{20}=5
  • Answer: 5ms^{-1} north
