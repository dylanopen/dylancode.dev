Ion specific charge

Examples

^{48}_{20}Ca is ionised by removing two electrons: find the specific charge of the ion

  • 20 protons
  • 20-2=18 electrons
  • 48 nucleon number (protons+neutrons)
  • Relative charge = 20-18=+2
  • Charge = 2\times1.6\times10^{-19}=3.2\times10^{-19}\,C
  • Relative mass = 48
  • Mass = 48\times1.67\times10^{-27}=8.016\times10^{-26}\,kg
    • Ignore the mass of electrons as they are so small.
  • Specific charge = \frac{3.2\times10^{-19}}{8.016\times10^{-26}}
  • Specific charge = 3.99\times10^{-20}
