Explaining light properties

There are some properties of light which can only be explained by certain models.

There are two main models of light:

  • The wave model
  • The particle (Photon) model

They can’t each explain everything, but together, they can:

PropertyWave modelParticle model
ReflectionThe light waves themselves are reflectedThe photon particles are reflected at the surface
RefractionThe light waves slow down and bend towards or away from the normalThe particles are just refracted at the surface
diffractionWaves spread out when they pass through a gapCannot explain it
InterferenceWaves superpose and their amplitudes add together at each point.Cannot explain it
polarisationThe direction of oscillation occurs in a certainCannot explain it
Photoelectric effectCannot explain itThe electrons have a threshold frequency based on their work function.

This shows that maybe light (and other waves and particles) aren’t just one or the other, but both - this is wave particle duality.
