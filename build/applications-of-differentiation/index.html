Applications of differentiation

There are a few things we can do with the derivative of a function, which are really useful for solving problems.

A reminder that use can use the power rule for differentiation or differentiation from first principles to calculate the derivative of a function.

Some things you can do with the derivative are:

  • Finding the tangent from differentiation at a point
  • Finding the normal from differentiation at a point
  • Finding finding stationary points from the derivative

finding the tangent from differentiationTo find the tangent at a point, calculate the derivative and evaluate it at that point to get the slope m, then use the point-slope formula y - y_1 = m(x - x_1).
finding the normal from differentiationThe normal is perpendicular to the tangent, so its slope is the negative reciprocal: m_{\text{normal}} = -\frac{1}{m_{\text{tangent}}}.
stationary points from derivativeStationary points occur where the derivative f'(x) = 0. These can be local maxima, minima, or points of inflection.
