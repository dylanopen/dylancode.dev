Hooke’s law

The extension of a spring is directly proportional to the force applied, until the limit of proportionality is reached.

Equation

We can write Hooke’s law, up until the limit of proportionality, as:

F=k\Delta L

  • F is the force applied, in newtons (N)
  • k is the spring constant (or stiffness constant) in newtons per metre (Nm^{-1})
  • \Delta L is the extension of the spring, in metres.

\text{force}=\text{spring constant}\times\text{extension}
