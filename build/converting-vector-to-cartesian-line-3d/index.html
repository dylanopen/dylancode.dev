Converting vector-form to cartesian-form line equations in 3D

Convert \pmatrix{7\\2\\3}=\lambda\pmatrix{0\\5\\2} to cartesian form

  • x=7+0\lambda \Rightarrow x=7
  • y=2+5\lambda \Rightarrow \lambda=\frac{y-2}{5}
  • z=3+2\lambda \Rightarrow \lambda=\\frac{z-3}{2}
  • Answer: x=7, \frac{y-2}{5}=\frac{z-3}{2}

Find the cartesian equation of the line between 2,3,-2) and (5,8,5)

  • Direction vector \vec d = \pmatrix{5-2\\8-3\\5-(-2)}=\pmatrix{3\\5\\7}
  • Vector equation: \vec r = \pmatrix{2\\3\\-2} + \lambda \pmatrix{3\\5\\7}
  • x=2+3\lambda \Rightarrow \lambda=\frac{x-2}{3}
  • y=3+5\lambda \Rightarrow \lambda=\frac{y-3}{5}
  • z=-2+7\lambda \Rightarrow \lambda=\frac{z+2}{7}
  • Answer: \frac{x-2}{3}=\frac{y-3}{5}=\frac{z+2}{7}
