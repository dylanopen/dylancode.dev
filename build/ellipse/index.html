Ellipse

An ellipse is just a stretch of a circle. Basically, a circle where the width does not equal the height, and the radius is different in the x and y directions.

Equation of an ellipse centred at the origin

\frac{x^2}{a^2} + \frac{y^2}{b^2} = 1

  • a is the distance from the centre to the edge of the ellipse in the x direction (half the width).
  • b is the distance from the centre to the edge of the ellipse in the y direction (half the height).

We can also define a and b as:

  • a is the (positive) x-intercept of the ellipse.
  • b is the (positive) y-intercept of the ellipse.

Asymptotes

Ellipses don’t have asymptotes (unlike some other conic sections) because they are closed curves.

As you move away from the centre of the ellipse, the curve will always loop back around and never approach a straight line.

Examples

Find the equation of the ellipse with x-intercept 3 and y-intercept 4

  • a = 3
  • b = 4
  • \frac{x^2}{3^2} + \frac{y^2}{4^2} = 1
  • \frac{x^2}{9} + \frac{y^2}{16} = 1

Find the equation of the ellipse with x-intercept 5 and y-intercept 2

  • a = 5
  • b = 2
  • \frac{x^2}{5^2} + \frac{y^2}{2^2} = 1
  • \frac{x^2}{25} + \frac{y^2}{4} = 1

An ellipse has the equation \frac{x^2}{16} + \frac{y^2}{9} = 1. Find the x-intercept and y-intercept

  • a^2 = 16 \Rightarrow a = 4
  • b^2 = 9 \Rightarrow b = 3
  • x-intercept: 4
  • y-intercept: 3
