Factorising a quadratic over complex numbersUse the quadratic formula x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a} with i = \sqrt{-1} to find complex roots, then write as a(x - \alpha)(x - \beta).
Factorising a cubic over complex numbers given one rootIf z = a is a root, divide the cubic by (z - a) using polynomial division to get a quadratic factor, then factorise that quadratic over complex numbers.
Factorising z^3 - 8 over complex numbersUsing the difference of cubes a^3 - b^3 = (a - b)(a^2 + ab + b^2), we get (z - 2)(z^2 + 2z + 4). Then solve z^2 + 2z + 4 = 0 using quadratic formula to get z = -1 \pm i\sqrt{3}, so fully factorised as (z - 2)(z + 1 - i\sqrt{3})(z + 1 + i\sqrt{3}).
Factorising z^3 + 27 over complex numbersUsing sum of cubes a^3 + b^3 = (a + b)(a^2 - ab + b^2), we get (z + 3)(z^2 - 3z + 9). Then solve z^2 - 3z + 9 = 0 to get z = \frac{3 \pm i3\sqrt{3}}{2}, so fully factorised as (z + 3)\left(z - \frac{3 + i3\sqrt{3}}{2}\right)\left(z - \frac{3 - i3\sqrt{3}}{2}\right).
Factorising a quartic over complex numbers when two roots are givenIf z = a and z = b are roots, the factors are (z - a)(z - b). Multiply these, then divide the original quartic by this quadratic product to find the remaining quadratic factor, then factor that further over complex numbers if needed.
Factorising z^4 - 4z^3 + 9z^2 - 4z + 8 given z = 2i and z = 1 + i are rootsSince coefficients are real, complex roots come in conjugate pairs, so z = -2i and z = 1 - i are also roots. The four linear factors are (z - 2i)(z + 2i)(z - 1 - i)(z - 1 + i) = (z^2 + 4)(z^2 - 2z + 2).
How to factorise z^4 - 1 over complex numbersRecognise as difference of squares: z^4 - 1 = (z^2 - 1)(z^2 + 1) = (z - 1)(z + 1)(z - i)(z + i).
