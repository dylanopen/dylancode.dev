Displacement vectors

Example

  • Let \vec{a} be the position vector of point A, where \vec{a} = \begin{pmatrix} 2 \\ 3 \end{pmatrix}.
  • Let \vec{b} be the position vector of point B, where \vec{b} = \begin{pmatrix} 5 \\ 7 \end{pmatrix}.

Find \vec{ab}

  • To get from \vec{a} to \vec{b}, we can subtract \vec{a} from \vec{b}:
  • \vec{ab} = \vec{b} - \vec{a}
  • \vec{ab} = \begin{pmatrix} 5 \\ 7 \end{pmatrix} - \begin{pmatrix} 2 \\ 3 \end{pmatrix}
  • \vec{ab} = \begin{pmatrix} 5 - 2 \\ 7 - 3 \end{pmatrix}
  • \vec{ab} = \begin{pmatrix} 3 \\ 4 \end{pmatrix}
