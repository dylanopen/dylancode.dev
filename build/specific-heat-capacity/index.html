Specific heat capacity

The specific heat capacity of a material is the amount of energy required to raise the temperature of 1 kilogram of a substance by 1 degree celsius.

Equation

From the definition above, we can find that:

\Delta E=m\,c\,\Delta\theta

  • \Delta E is the change in energy, i.e. how much energy was gained/lost, measured in joules.
  • m is the mass of the substance, measured in kilograms.
  • c is the specific heat capacity of the substance, measured in J\,kg^{-1}\,^\circ C^{-1}.
  • \Delta \theta is the change in temperature, in degree celsius.
