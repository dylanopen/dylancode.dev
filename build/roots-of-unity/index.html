Roots of unity

Roots of z^n=1

The nth roots of unity, for z^n=1, are:

1,e^\frac{2\pi i}n,e^\frac{4\pi i}n,e^\frac{6\pi i}n,e^\frac{2(n-1)\pi i}n

Sum of roots of unity

The sum of roots of unity are always zero.

Examples

Factorise and solve z^3=1

  • z^3-1=0.
  • (z-1) is a factor as z=1 is a root.
  • z^3-1=(z-1)(z^2+z+1)=0 by inspection.
  • z=1 is one root.
  • z=\frac{-1\pm\sqrt{1^2-4\cdot1\cdot1}}{2\cdot1}
  • z=-\frac12\pm\frac{\sqrt3}2i
  • Roots:
    • z=1
    • z=-\frac12+\frac{\sqrt3}2i
    • z=-\frac12-\frac{\sqrt3}2i

Solve z^5=1

  • |z^5|=1
  • |z|=1
  • \arg z^5=0,2\pi,4\pi,5\pi,8\pi,...
  • \arg z=0,\frac25\pi,\frac45\pi,\frac65\pi,\frac85\pi
  • so z=e^{i\cdot k\cdot\frac25\pi} in exponential form
    • for k=0\text{ to }4
